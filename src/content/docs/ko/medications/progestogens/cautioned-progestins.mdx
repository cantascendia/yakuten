Medroxyprogesterone acetate (MPA) is **not recommended by WPATH SOC 8** for transfeminine HRT<CitationRef id="coleman-2022" num={1} authors="Coleman E et al." year={2022} title="Standards of Care for the Health of Transgender and Gender Diverse People, Version 8" />. This page explains why and helps you understand the reasons to choose safer alternatives.

## What Is MPA?

Medroxyprogesterone acetate (MPA) is a synthetic progestin, commonly known by brand names:

- **Provera** (oral, 5-10 mg)
- **Depo-Provera** (injection, 150 mg/3 months)

MPA was widely used in postmenopausal hormone therapy but large clinical trials (WHI study) revealed significant risks.

## Why It Is Not Recommended

### 1. Significantly Increases VTE Risk

Compared to natural progesterone, MPA significantly increases venous thromboembolism (VTE) risk<CitationRef id="canonico-2018" num={2} authors="Canonico M et al." year={2018} title="Hormone therapy and venous thromboembolism" /><CitationRef id="vinogradova-2019" num={3} authors="Vinogradova Y et al." year={2019} title="Use of hormone replacement therapy and risk of venous thromboembolism" />:

- MPA + estrogen VTE risk: RR approximately 2.0-2.5
- Natural progesterone + estrogen VTE risk: RR approximately 1.0 (no significant increase)

### 2. Adverse Metabolic Effects

- May reduce HDL ("good" cholesterol)
- Unfavorable effects on insulin resistance
- Weight gain

### 3. Safer Alternatives Exist

- [Micronized progesterone](/en/medications/progestogens/progesterone/): Natural/bioidentical, VTE risk neutral
- [Dydrogesterone](/en/medications/progestogens/dydrogesterone/): Better oral bioavailability, superior safety data
- [Hydroxyprogesterone caproate](/en/medications/progestogens/hydroxyprogesterone/): Injectable option

## If You Are Currently Using MPA

<WarningBox title="Do Not Stop Abruptly">
If you are currently using MPA (oral or injection), do not discontinue abruptly on your own. Discuss a gradual transition to a safer progestogen alternative with your healthcare provider. Sudden discontinuation of hormonal medications can cause hormonal fluctuations and discomfort.
</WarningBox>

## Exception Circumstances

In resource-limited settings where MPA may be the only available progestogen:

- Use the lowest possible dose
- Intensify VTE risk factor monitoring
- Prioritize not smoking and weight management
- Switch to natural progesterone or dydrogesterone as soon as available
