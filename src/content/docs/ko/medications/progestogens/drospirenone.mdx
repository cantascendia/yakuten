Drospirenone is a synthetic progestogen structurally derived from spironolactone, possessing simultaneous **anti-androgenic** and **anti-mineralocorticoid** (spironolactone-like) activity. A non-mainstream option in transfeminine HRT, its unique pharmacological profile may be valuable in select scenarios<CitationRef id="coleman-2022" num={1} authors="Coleman E et al." year={2022} title="Standards of Care for the Health of Transgender and Gender Diverse People, Version 8" />.
</DrugInfoBox>

## Mechanism

Drospirenone has multiple pharmacological activities:

- **Progestogenic activity**: Binds progesterone receptors
- **Anti-androgenic activity**: Competitive androgen receptor binding (similar mechanism to spironolactone)
- **Anti-mineralocorticoid activity**: Spironolactone-like diuretic effect
- **No estrogenic or glucocorticoid activity**

### Unique Positioning

Drospirenone is the **only progestogen that combines anti-androgenic and anti-mineralocorticoid activity**. For users seeking both progestogenic effects and mild anti-androgenic coverage, drospirenone may reduce the need for a separate anti-androgen.

<WarningBox title="Usage Caution">
Most drospirenone-containing products are combined oral contraceptives (e.g. Yaz, Yasmin) that contain **ethinylestradiol (EE)**. This site [explicitly prohibits](/en/medications/banned-drugs/) ethinylestradiol in HRT. If choosing drospirenone, you must use a **drospirenone-only formulation** (e.g. Slynd 4 mg), not a combined EE product.
</WarningBox>

## Monitoring

- **K+ (potassium)**: Drospirenone has spironolactone-like potassium-sparing effects; monitor potassium
- **PRL**: Every 6 months
- If co-administered with spironolactone, hyperkalemia risk increases significantly

<DangerBox title="Safety Limits">
- **Must not be combined with spironolactone**: Both have potassium-sparing effects; combination may cause dangerous hyperkalemia<CitationRef id="hembree-2017" num={2} authors="Hembree WC et al." year={2017} title="Endocrine Treatment of Gender-Dysphoric/Gender-Incongruent Persons" />
- **Must not use EE-containing formulations** (Yaz/Yasmin etc.)
- Use with caution in renal impairment
- No large-scale safety data for drospirenone in transgender HRT exists
</DangerBox>
