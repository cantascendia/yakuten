The following classifications or sources of estrogen **must absolutely never be used** for transgender HRT. Their risks violently outweigh any theoretical benefits. Even if you are completely cut off from legitimate medical pathways, resorting to these alternatives can inflict permanent, fatal damage.

---

## Ethinylestradiol (EE)

<DangerBox title="Ethinylestradiol — 20x The VTE Risk of Bioidentical E2">

**Ethinylestradiol (EE)** is a synthetic estrogen predominantly found in combined oral contraceptive pills (birth control) such as Diane-35. It fundamentally differs from bioidentical estradiol (E2) in its chemical structure and metabolic pathway.

**Why it is dangerous**:

- EE hammers the liver's coagulation factors infinitely harder than natural E2, skyrocketing VTE (blood clot) risks to roughly **6 to 20 times higher** than swallowing standard bioidentical E2<CitationRef id="canonico-2018" num={1} authors="Canonico M et al." year={2018} title="Hormone therapy and venous thromboembolism: an updated overview" /><CitationRef id="vinogradova-2019" num={2} authors="Vinogradova Y et al." year={2019} title="Use of hormone replacement therapy and risk of venous thromboembolism" />.
- Early historical trans HRT regimens heavily utilized EE, which directly resulted in the notoriously high rates of cardiovascular death and pulmonary embolisms associated with early trans healthcare<CitationRef id="deblok-2021" num={3} authors="de Blok CJM et al." year={2021} title="Amsterdam cohort 50-year follow-up" />.
- Both WPATH SOC 8 and the Endocrine Society 2017 Guidelines **explicitly ban** the use of Ethinylestradiol in gender-affirming hormone therapy<CitationRef id="hembree-2017" num={4} authors="Hembree WC et al." year={2017} title="Endocrine Treatment of Gender-Dysphoric/Gender-Incongruent Persons" /><CitationRef id="coleman-2022" num={5} authors="Coleman E et al." year={2022} title="Standards of Care for the Health of Transgender and Gender Diverse People, Version 8" />.

**Birth Control is NOT HRT**:

Formulations like Diane-35 contain 35 μg of EE alongside 2 mg of Cyproterone Acetate (CPA). Because it contains CPA (an anti-androgen), many desperate DIY users globally attempt to use it as an all-in-one HRT pill. This is an **incredibly dangerous misuse**.
- 35 μg of EE carries clot risks that shatter any acceptable safety margin.
- CPA alone is already pro-coagulant. Stacking it with EE amplifies the death risk exponentially.
- The actual estrogenic feminizing power of Diane-35 is remarkably weak compared to bioidentical E2, meaning you are accepting lethal clot risks for subpar feminization.

**If you are currently taking Diane-35 or any birth control as HRT:** Stop immediately and switch to bioidentical estradiol (Estrace, Progynova) and dose your anti-androgens separately.
</DangerBox>

## Conjugated Equine Estrogens (Premarin)

<DangerBox title="Conjugated Equine Estrogens — High Risk, Low Reward">

**Conjugated Equine Estrogens (CEE)**, most famously branded as Premarin, is a cocktail of estrogens extracted from the urine of pregnant mares.

**Why it is dangerous**:

- CEE carries a tremendously elevated VTE risk compared to bioidentical E2<CitationRef id="vinogradova-2019" num={2} authors="Vinogradova Y et al." year={2019} title="Use of hormone replacement therapy and risk of venous thromboembolism" />.
- The massive Women's Health Initiative (WHI) study definitively proved that oral CEE significantly increased the incidence of blood clots and cardiovascular events<CitationRef id="hembree-2017" num={4} authors="Hembree WC et al." year={2017} title="Endocrine Treatment of Gender-Dysphoric/Gender-Incongruent Persons" />.
- It contains horse-specific estrogen analogues (like equilin) that human bodies do not metabolize predictably, causing wild disruptions to the coagulation system.
- Modern clinical guidelines strictly advise the use of **17β-Estradiol** (natural E2) rather than CEE<CitationRef id="hembree-2017" num={4} authors="Hembree WC et al." year={2017} />.

**If you are currently taking Premarin:** Demand that your doctor switch you to bioidentical estradiol valerate or hemihydrate.
</DangerBox>

## Veterinary Hormones

<DangerBox title="Animal-Grade Hormones — Unknown Formulations, Uncontrollable Doses">

In some regions lacking medical access, individuals occasionally resort to agricultural or veterinary hormone medications (such as injectable estrogens intended for livestock). This practice is **catastrophically dangerous**.

**Why it is dangerous**:

- **Unknown Estrogens**: Veterinary formulas often rely on cheap, hyper-potent synthetic estrogens (like diethylstilbestrol) rather than bioidentical E2.
- **Uncontrollable Dosing**: These drugs are concentrated for animals weighing a ton. A microscopic dosing error will result in massive human overdose.
- **Impure Manufacturing**: Agricultural drugs do not adhere to human pharmaceutical standards. They contain impurities, heavy metals, and excipients completely unfit for human biology.
- **Sterility Issues**: Veterinary injectables lack human-grade sterility safeguards, making bacterial sepsis incredibly likely.

**There is zero clinical safety data regarding human consumption of veterinary hormones.** Do not use them.
</DangerBox>

## Unregulated Homebrew (DIY) Injectables

<DangerBox title="Homebrew Injectables — The Sterility Gamble">

Because branded injectable estradiol is frequently expensive or banned (such as the widespread online sales bans in parts of Asia), a massive global "homebrew" market exists. Individuals purchase raw estradiol powder from overseas chemical suppliers and cook their own injectable vials at home.

**Why it is dangerous**:

- **Sterility Failure**: Injectables must be formulated in ISO-certified cleanrooms under laminar flow hoods. A kitchen counter cannot achieve this. Injecting contaminated fluid directly into your body leads to severe abscesses, systemic sepsis, and potentially death.
- **Lack of Purity Verification**: "Raw E2 powder" bought from random decentralized suppliers could be cut with literally anything. Without High-Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC) testing, you have no idea what you are injecting.
- **Toxic Oils & Solvents**: Injectable hormones require specific carrier oils (like castor or MCT oil) and solvents (like benzyl benzoate) mixed at precise mathematical ratios. Poor mixing causes the E2 to crash out of the solution into crystals, or triggers severe anaphylactic allergic reactions.

**Homebrewed injectables are not merely "suboptimal"—a bad batch can kill you.**

**If you must DIY:** Stick to factory-sealed, commercially manufactured pills (like Progynova/Estrace), which are significantly harder to contaminate and easier to verify.
</DangerBox>
