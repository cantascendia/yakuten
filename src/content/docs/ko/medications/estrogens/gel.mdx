## The Safety Advantage

Like patches, transdermal gel bypasses the digestive system and the liver entirely. This means:

- **Minimal VTE Risk:** The relative risk of blood clots is virtually identical to the unmedicated population (RR ≈ 0.97)<CitationRef id="canonico-2018" num={1} authors="Canonico M et al." year={2018} title="Hormone therapy and venous thromboembolism: an updated overview" />, making it vastly safer than oral routes.
- **Low Liver Burden:** Standard hepatic metabolism is bypassed, sparing liver function<CitationRef id="hembree-2017" num={2} authors="Hembree WC et al." year={2017} title="Endocrine Treatment of Gender-Dysphoric/Gender-Incongruent Persons" />.
- **Ideal for High-Risk Users:** Strongly recommended for users with higher BMI, older age, or a family history of coagulation disorders<CitationRef id="coleman-2022" num={3} authors="Coleman E et al." year={2022} title="Standards of Care for the Health of Transgender and Gender Diverse People, Version 8" />.

## Application Guide

<img src="/images/diagrams/ko/gel-application.webp" alt="젤 도포 부위도: 상완 안쪽, 허벅지 안쪽, 복부 권장, 가슴 부위 금지" width="800" height="500" loading="lazy" decoding="async" style="width:100%;height:auto;border-radius:4px;margin:0 0 var(--space-lg);" />

1. **Prep the Skin:** Ensure the skin is entirely dry, clean, and free of cuts, rashes, or other lotions.
2. **Select the Site:** Recommended areas include the inner arms, inner thighs, or abdomen. **Never apply it to the breasts or chest.**
3. **Application:** Spread the gel in a thin, even layer across a reasonably large surface area (about the size of 1-2 handprints). Gently rub it in; do not aggressively massage it.
4. **Drying Time:** Wait 3-5 minutes for the gel to dry completely before putting clothes on over the area.
5. **Avoid Contact:** Do not let the applied area touch anyone else's skin (partners, pets, children) until it has been washed or covered.
6. **Wash Hands:** Vigorously wash your hands with soap and water immediately afterward.

### Best Practices

- **Apply at the same time every day** to stabilize blood concentration curves.
- **Rotate your application sites** daily to prevent localized skin irritation.
- **Do not swim, bathe, or intensely sweat** for at least 1 hour post-application.
- **Do not mix skincare:** Applying sunscreens or heavy moisturizers directly over or under the gel can create physical barriers that ruin absorption.
- **If you miss a dose:** Apply it as soon as you remember. If it is almost time for your next dose, skip the missed one entirely. **Do not double your dose.**

## When to Draw Blood

For users on daily gel, blood should ideally be drawn **4-6 hours after application**, which captures the pharmacokinetic steady-state. Alternatively, you can measure right before your next application (the trough), much like oral pills<CitationRef id="hembree-2017" num={2} authors="Hembree WC et al." year={2017} />.

Whichever method you choose, **stick to it rigidly** for every future lab test so your data remains comparable.

<WarningBox title="Absorption is Highly Variable">
The real-world absorption rate of transdermal gel fluctuates wildly between individuals based on physiological and environmental factors:

- **Skin Condition:** Dry, heavily keratinized skin absorbs poorly; thinner, hydrated skin absorbs optimally.
- **Application Site:** Inner arms/thighs yield far higher absorption than outer, thicker skin barriers.
- **Surface Area:** Spreading it too thick over a tiny patch of skin chokes the absorption rate.
- **Environment:** High heat and humidity can cause you to sweat off the gel before it truly penetrates the dermal layers.

Because of this, you cannot judge your progress by the number of pumps/mg you take. You must rely on bloodwork. If you are struggling to hit 50 pg/mL even while slathering on 3-4 mg a day, you may just be a "poor absorber" and need to switch to patches, pills, or injections.
</WarningBox>

## Global Access

Aesthetic estradiol gel (like Estrogel or Oestrogel) is standard first-line treatment in much of the UK (via the NHS) and Europe, owing to its elite safety profile. It heavily favored in countries with strict public healthcare protocols prioritizing harm reduction. In the global DIY space, it is moderately accessible.
