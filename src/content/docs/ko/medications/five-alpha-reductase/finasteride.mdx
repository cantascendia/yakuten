Finasteride is a selective type II 5α-reductase inhibitor that reduces DHT levels by approximately 65-70% by blocking the conversion of testosterone to dihydrotestosterone. Used as adjunctive therapy in transfeminine HRT, primarily for hair loss and excessive body hair.
</DrugInfoBox>

## Mechanism

Finasteride selectively inhibits **type II** 5α-reductase (primarily found in hair follicles and prostate), reducing serum DHT by ~65-70% while having virtually no effect on testosterone levels.

Compared to [dutasteride](/en/medications/five-alpha-reductase/dutasteride/), finasteride inhibits only one isoform, resulting in less DHT reduction but also potentially fewer side effects.

- Start at 1 mg/day, the standard hair loss dose
- 5 mg tablets (Proscar) are often cheaper and can be quarter-split
- Onset typically requires **3-6 months**

<WarningBox title="Finasteride Does Not Replace Anti-Androgens">
Finasteride only lowers DHT, not testosterone. It cannot replace CPA, spironolactone, or GnRH agonists. If your T levels remain above target, adjust your primary anti-androgen regimen rather than adding a 5αRI<CitationRef id="hembree-2017" num={1} authors="Hembree WC et al." year={2017} title="Endocrine Treatment of Gender-Dysphoric/Gender-Incongruent Persons" />.
</WarningBox>

## Monitoring

- **T**: Continue routine HRT monitoring every 3 months
- **DHT** (optional): Can confirm 5αRI effect
- **Liver function**: Finasteride rarely causes hepatic injury; no additional monitoring usually needed
- **PSA**: Finasteride reduces PSA by ~50%; inform your doctor if PSA is being tested

## Contraindications

- Finasteride allergy
- Severe hepatic impairment
- Pregnant partners must not handle crushed/broken tablets (teratogenic to male fetuses — typically not relevant in trans HRT context)

<DangerBox title="Important Notes">
- **Effects reverse on discontinuation**: DHT returns to pre-treatment levels within weeks after stopping
- Do not stop other HRT medications because hair loss improves
- If no improvement after 6 months, finasteride may not be effective for you — discuss switching to [dutasteride](/en/medications/five-alpha-reductase/dutasteride/) with your provider
</DangerBox>
