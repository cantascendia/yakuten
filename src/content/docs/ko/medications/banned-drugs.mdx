## Ethinylestradiol (EE) / Diane-35

<DangerBox title="Ethinylestradiol — The Ultimate Clot Risk">

**Ethinylestradiol (EE)** is a highly synthetic estrogen primarily used in combined birth control pills (like Diane-35). 

**Why it is dangerous**:
- EE hammers the liver's coagulation factors, rocketing VTE (blood clot) risks to roughly **6 to 20 times higher** than taking standard bioidentical E2<CitationRef id="canonico-2018" num={1} authors="Canonico M et al." year={2018} title="Hormone therapy and venous thromboembolism: an updated overview" /><CitationRef id="vinogradova-2019" num={2} authors="Vinogradova Y et al." year={2019} title="Use of hormone replacement therapy and risk of venous thromboembolism" />.
- It directly caused the historically high rates of pulmonary embolisms and deep vein thrombosis seen in early trans healthcare<CitationRef id="deblok-2021" num={3} authors="de Blok CJM et al." year={2021} title="Amsterdam cohort 50-year follow-up" />.
- WPATH SOC 8 and Endocrine Society 2017 Guidelines universally **ban** the use of EE in GAHT<CitationRef id="hembree-2017" num={4} authors="Hembree WC et al." year={2017} title="Endocrine Treatment of Gender-Dysphoric/Gender-Incongruent Persons" /><CitationRef id="coleman-2022" num={5} authors="Coleman E et al." year={2022} title="Standards of Care for the Health of Transgender and Gender Diverse People, Version 8" />.

</DangerBox>

### Birth Control is NOT HRT

Medications like Diane-35 contain 35 μg of EE alongside 2 mg of Cyproterone (CPA). Desperate DIY users frequently take Diane-35 under the delusion that it is a cheap, all-in-one HRT pill. **It is not.** 
- The CPA in the drug is already pro-coagulant. Stacking it with the lethally pro-coagulant EE amplifies the death risk exponentially.
- The feminization power of Diane-35 is incredibly weak compared to standard bioidentical estrogen.

### If you are currently taking Diane-35
Switch to oral bioidentical estradiol (Progynova / Estrace) immediately and source your anti-androgens (CPA, Spiro, Bica) separately. Do not cold turkey your blockers without having a replacement plan.

## Conjugated Equine Estrogens (Premarin)

<DangerBox title="Conjugated Equine Estrogens — High Risk, Low Reward">

**Conjugated Equine Estrogens (CEE)**, most famously branded as Premarin, is a cocktail of estrogens extracted from the urine of pregnant mares.

**Why it is dangerous**:
- The massive Women's Health Initiative (WHI) study definitively proved that oral CEE significantly spiked cardiovascular events and clot rates<CitationRef id="hembree-2017" num={4} authors="Hembree WC et al." year={2017} />.
- CEE carries a tremendously elevated VTE risk compared to bioidentical E2<CitationRef id="vinogradova-2019" num={2} authors="Vinogradova Y et al." year={2019} />.
- The human body cannot predictably metabolize horse-specific estrogen analogues (like equilin).

</DangerBox>

## Medroxyprogesterone Acetate (MPA / Provera)

<DangerBox title="MPA — Do Not Use Synethetic Progestins">

**Medroxyprogesterone Acetate (MPA)** is a highly synthetic progestin. It was heavily used in previous decades but has since been largely abandoned by modern medicine regarding HRT.

**Why it is dangerous**:
- In the WHI study, patients taking CEE + MPA experienced a horrifying spike in invasive breast cancer compared to those just taking CEE<CitationRef id="hembree-2017" num={4} authors="Hembree WC et al." year={2017} />.
- MPA causes significantly worse blood clot risks compared to natural bioidentical progesterone<CitationRef id="canonico-2018" num={1} authors="Canonico M et al." year={2018} />.
- It possesses glucocorticoid activity, meaning prolonged use violently alters your baseline metabolic functions.

</DangerBox>

### If you are currently taking MPA
Demand a prescription for **Bioidentical Micronized Progesterone** (Prometrium / Utrogestan). You will lose nothing regarding feminization and will drastically lower your cancer and VTE risks.

## Veterinary Hormones

<DangerBox title="Animal-Grade Hormones — Unknown Formulations, Uncontrollable Doses">

In some regions lacking medical access, individuals occasionally resort to agricultural or veterinary hormone medications (such as injectable estrogens intended for livestock reproduction). This practice is **catastrophically dangerous**.

**Why it is dangerous**:
- **Unknown Estrogens**: Veterinary formulas often rely on cheap, hyper-potent synthetic estrogens like diethylstilbestrol (which is heavily regulated or banned in humans due to extreme cancer risks).
- **Uncontrollable Dosing**: An injectable meant to jumpstart a 2,000-pound cow cannot be safely divided for a human. Microscopic math errors yield massive human overdoses.
- **Impure Manufacturing**: Agricultural drugs do not adhere to human pharmaceutical standards. They contain impurities, heavy metals, and excipients completely unfit for human biology.
- **Sterility Issues**: Veterinary injectables lack human-grade sterility safeguards, making bacterial sepsis incredibly likely.

</DangerBox>

## Unregulated Homebrew (DIY) Injectables

<DangerBox title="Homebrew Injectables — The Sterility Gamble">

Because branded injectable estradiol is frequently expensive or heavily restricted by governments, a massive global "homebrew" market exists. Individuals purchase raw estradiol powder from overseas chemical suppliers and cook their own injectable vials at home.

**Why it is dangerous**:
- **Sterility Failure**: Injectables must be formulated in ISO-certified cleanrooms under laminar flow hoods. A kitchen counter cannot achieve this. Injecting contaminated fluid directly into your deep muscle leads to severe abscesses, systemic blood poisoning (sepsis), and potentially death.
- **Lack of Purity Verification**: "Raw E2 powder" bought from random decentralized suppliers could be cut with literally anything. Without High-Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC) testing, you have no idea what you are injecting.
- **Toxic Oils & Solvents**: Injectable hormones require specific carrier oils (like castor or MCT oil) and solvents (like benzyl benzoate) mixed at precise mathematical ratios. Poor mixing causes the E2 to crash out of the solution into crystals, or triggers severe anaphylactic allergic reactions.
- **Pyrogens**: Unfiltered homebrews are loaded with pyrogens (endotoxins) that trigger violent, uncontrollable fever responses upon injection.

</DangerBox>

### What to do if DIY is your only option:
If your governmental reality completely cuts you off from legal HRT, do not resort to kitchen-sink injectables. **Stick to factory-sealed, commercially manufactured oral pills** (like Progynova/Estrace/Estrofem) acquired via the gray market. Oral pills are significantly harder to contaminate and infinitely easier to verify for authenticity compared to random vats of oil.
