## Mission

**Evidence-Based · Harm Reduction · Guiding You to Care**

HRT Yakuten is a safety-baseline information site for transgender women in the Chinese-speaking community who use HRT. Our goal is not to replace doctors, but to help those who are already taking — or about to start — hormone therapy:

- **Understand your blood test results** and know which abnormal values warrant a medical visit
- **Learn the real risks of medications**, rather than relying on community hearsay and unverified anecdotes
- **Recognize emergency warning signs** (DVT, liver damage, meningioma) and make the right call at critical moments
- **Find trans-friendly medical resources** and transition from DIY to supervised medical care

### What this site is NOT

- ❌ Not an encyclopedia or medical textbook
- ❌ Not a forum, community, or social platform
- ❌ Not a drug purchasing channel or pharmacy recommendation site
- ❌ Does not provide personalized dosing advice ("you should take X mg")

## Content Sources

All medical content on this site is based on the following international clinical guidelines and peer-reviewed literature:

| Source | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Endocrine Society 2017** | *Endocrine Treatment of Gender-Dysphoric/Gender-Incongruent Persons* — Clinical Practice Guideline |
| **WPATH SOC 8 (2022)** | *Standards of Care for the Health of Transgender and Gender Diverse People*, Version 8 |
| **UCSF Transgender Care** | University of California, San Francisco — Transgender Care Guidelines |
| **EMA (2020)** | European Medicines Agency restrictions and safety assessment on CPA |
| **PubMed / PMC** | Peer-reviewed original research papers (each citation includes a DOI) |

The superscript numbers next to each medical recommendation (e.g. <sup>[1]</sup>) link to the specific reference. **No citation = no content** — this is an ironclad rule of this site.

## Content Review Process

| Step | Standard |
|------|----------|
| **Citation requirement** | Every clinical recommendation must trace back to at least one peer-reviewed paper or international guideline |
| **Cross-validation** | Drug dosage ranges must be cross-validated against ≥2 independent sources (e.g. WPATH + Endocrine Society + UCSF) |
| **Evidence grading** | Each page is labeled with an evidence level: A (multiple RCTs/Meta-analyses), B (single RCT/cohort), C (case reports/expert opinion), X (no evidence) |
| **Review date** | Each page displays its last reviewed date at the bottom |
| **Update cycle** | Content is reviewed quarterly for currency and updated based on newly published literature |

<WarningBox title="Content Limitations">
This site is currently maintained by an individual and has not undergone formal peer review by a professional medical editor. We make every effort to ensure accuracy, but errors and omissions are possible. If you find any inaccuracies, please submit an Issue on GitHub.
</WarningBox>

## Privacy

**This site does not collect or store any personal data.**

| Category | Measure |
|----------|---------|
| **Blood test tool** | Pure front-end JavaScript calculation — your input data never leaves your browser and is never sent to any server |
| **AI Q&A** | Conversations are processed in real time via Edge Functions — nothing is stored, logged, or used for training |
| **Analytics** | Self-hosted Umami analytics — only page view counts, no IP addresses or personal identifiers collected |
| **Tracking** | Zero third-party tracking scripts — no Google Analytics, no embedded social media trackers |
| **Cookies** | Used only for theme preference (dark/light mode), not for tracking |
| **Ads** | None, and there never will be |

## Disclaimer

The content on this site is **for educational and informational purposes only** and does not constitute medical advice.

- This site cannot replace a face-to-face diagnosis and treatment by a qualified physician
- The use, adjustment, and discontinuation of medications should be carried out under the guidance of a qualified healthcare professional
- If you experience any emergency symptoms (unilateral leg swelling/pain, sudden chest pain, jaundice, etc.), stop medication immediately and go to the nearest emergency room
- This site assumes no responsibility for any medical consequences resulting from the use of its content

**Self-medication (DIY) carries real risks.** This site aims to help you use medications more safely, but the safest approach is always to undergo HRT under the supervision of a doctor.
