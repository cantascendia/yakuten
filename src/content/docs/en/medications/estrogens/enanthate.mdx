Estradiol enanthate (EEn) is a medium-to-long-acting injectable estradiol ester with a half-life between estradiol valerate (EV) and estradiol cypionate (EC), at approximately 5-7 days<CitationRef id="oriowo-1980" num={1} authors="Oriowo MA et al." year={1980} title="Pharmacokinetics of Estradiol Esters" />. It is commonly found as a component of combined injectable contraceptives (e.g. Perlutal) and can also be used as a standalone HRT medication.
</DrugInfoBox>

## Pharmacokinetics

Pharmacokinetic profile of estradiol enanthate<CitationRef id="oriowo-1980" num={1} authors="Oriowo MA et al." year={1980} title="Pharmacokinetics of Estradiol Esters" /><CitationRef id="kuhl-2005" num={2} authors="Kuhl H" year={2005} title="Pharmacology of estrogens and progestogens" />:

- **Half-life**: ~5-7 days
- **Peak time**: 3-5 days post-injection
- **Trough time**: 7-10 days post-injection
- **Steady state**: Reached after ~3-4 regular cycles

<WarningBox title="Availability Note">
Estradiol enanthate is not widely available in many regions. It is primarily found in Latin America (as a component of Perlutal) and select European countries. If you need injectable estradiol, [estradiol valerate](/en/medications/estrogens/injection/) is more accessible in most regions.
</WarningBox>

## Monitoring

- **E2**: Every 3 months, trough draw (morning before injection). Target 100-200 pg/mL
- **T**: Every 3 months. Target &lt;50 ng/dL
- **Liver function (ALT/AST)**: Baseline + every 6 months

## Injection Technique

Injection procedure is identical to [estradiol valerate injection](/en/medications/estrogens/injection/#injection-technique).

<DangerBox title="Safety Limits">
- Same safety limits apply as other injectable estradiol esters
- Single injection dose must not exceed 10 mg
- E2 >200 pg/mL does not enhance feminization, only increases risk<CitationRef id="hembree-2017" num={4} authors="Hembree WC et al." year={2017} title="Endocrine Treatment of Gender-Dysphoric/Gender-Incongruent Persons" />
- If using a combined formulation (e.g. Perlutal), the progestogen component may carry additional risks; consult a physician
</DangerBox>
