Estradiol cypionate (EC) is a long-acting injectable estradiol ester, primarily used in the Americas. Compared to estradiol valerate (EV), EC has a longer half-life (~8-10 days vs 4-5 days), allowing for extended injection intervals<CitationRef id="oriowo-1980" num={1} authors="Oriowo MA et al." year={1980} title="Pharmacokinetics of Estradiol Esters" />. Brand name: Depo-Estradiol.
</DrugInfoBox>

## Pharmacokinetics

Pharmacokinetic profile of estradiol cypionate<CitationRef id="oriowo-1980" num={1} authors="Oriowo MA et al." year={1980} title="Pharmacokinetics of Estradiol Esters" /><CitationRef id="kuhl-2005" num={2} authors="Kuhl H" year={2005} title="Pharmacology of estrogens and progestogens" />:

- **Half-life**: ~8-10 days (vs ~4-5 days for EV)
- **Peak time**: 2-4 days post-injection
- **Trough time**: 7-10 days post-injection
- **Steady state**: Reached after ~3-4 regular cycles

Due to EC's longer half-life, some users opt for 10-14 day injection intervals.

<WarningBox title="Injection Interval & Blood Draw Timing">
EC users should have blood drawn on the **morning before the next injection** to measure trough levels. If using a 10-day schedule, trough is on the morning of day 10. Target trough E2: 100-200 pg/mL.
</WarningBox>

EC is primarily available in the US and Latin America. In regions where it is unavailable, [estradiol valerate injection](/en/medications/estrogens/injection/) is the standard alternative.

## Monitoring

Same as other injectable estradiol esters:

- **E2**: Every 3 months, trough draw (morning before injection). Target 100-200 pg/mL
- **T**: Every 3 months. Target &lt;50 ng/dL
- **Liver function (ALT/AST)**: Baseline + every 6 months

## Injection Technique

Injection procedure is identical to [estradiol valerate injection](/en/medications/estrogens/injection/#injection-technique). Both SC and IM routes are suitable.

<DangerBox title="Safety Limits">
- **Single injection dose must not exceed 10 mg**
- The goal is to maintain stable physiological E2 levels, not to chase high peaks
- E2 >200 pg/mL does not produce stronger feminization, only increased clot risk<CitationRef id="hembree-2017" num={4} authors="Hembree WC et al." year={2017} title="Endocrine Treatment of Gender-Dysphoric/Gender-Incongruent Persons" />
- If EC is not available in your region, do not purchase through unverified channels
</DangerBox>
